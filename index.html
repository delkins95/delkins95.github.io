Independent Study

Throughout my Senior year at the College of Wooster, I have taken what I have learned in and out of the classroom, and applied it to a yearlong independent project. As a student-athlete at the College of Wooster, I have always had a passion for collegiate athletics and its role following graduation. Using basic Economic theory, I have developed the hypothesis that student-athletes have greater productivity than non-student-athletes, leading to greater success in the workforce. After surveying over 600 former College of Wooster alumni, I was able to use econometric analysis to support my hypothesis, with findings showing that despite spending less hours studying, student-athletes did in fact have a higher starting salary than non-student-athletes.
